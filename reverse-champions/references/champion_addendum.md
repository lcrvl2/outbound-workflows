# Champion Addendum

Append this section to the GTM playbook when generating reverse-champion emails. These rules override or extend the base playbook.

## Champion Angle

You are writing to prospects at companies where a **former employee** (the "champion") is now a closed-won customer using Agorapulse. The champion's success at their current company lends credibility to the outreach.

## Privacy Rules (Non-Negotiable)

| Rule | Specification |
|------|--------------|
| Champion name | NEVER mention by name |
| Champion current role | NEVER mention their title or current position |
| Champion current company | NEVER mention where they work now |
| Allowed reference | "someone who used to work at {{companyName}}" or "a former {{companyName}} team member" |
| Multiple champions | "multiple people who've worked at {{companyName}} before" |

### Allowed Phrasings

- "someone who used to work at {{companyName}} now relies on us for..."
- "a former {{companyName}} team member switched to our platform and..."
- "we work with people who've been in your exact position at {{companyName}}"

### Forbidden Phrasings

- "Your former colleague [Name] recommended..."
- "[Name], who used to be [Title] at {{companyName}}..."
- "Someone at [Champion's Current Company] suggested..."
- Any detail that could identify the specific champion

## Three-Email Structure (Champion Angle)

### Email 1: "The Insider" (New Thread)

- Subject line: 2-4 words, segment-relevant (NOT "former colleague" or "champion")
- Open with the reality of their situation (team scaling, platform complexity, tool sprawl)
- Weave in that someone who used to work at their company now uses Agorapulse
- Frame the champion's success as proof that teams like theirs benefit
- Soft interest check CTA

### Email 2: "The Proof Point" (Same Thread, No Subject)

- Short case study aligned with their industry/size
- Shows concrete outcomes, not features
- Implicitly connects to their situation
- No explicit CTA - proof speaks for itself

### Email 3: "Social Proof + FOMO" (New Thread)

- New subject line, different angle
- Frame: "Companies like [champion's CW company] are already doing X"
- Create urgency through industry momentum, not artificial pressure
- Easy out or easy in CTA
- NO champion intro offer (too time-consuming to coordinate)

## Multi-Champion Handling

When multiple CW contacts previously worked at the same target company:

- Use plural phrasing: "multiple people who've worked at {{companyName}}"
- Emphasize the pattern: "it's not a coincidence that several former {{companyName}} team members ended up choosing us"
- This is a stronger signal - lean into it

## Hybrid Personalization

- **Email body**: Generated per target company (same champion story for all contacts at that company)
- **Subject line + opening sentence**: Personalized per contact (their name, their title)
- This balances quality with cost efficiency

## What NOT to Do

- Don't position the email as a referral or introduction
- Don't promise the champion will vouch for you or make an intro
- Don't reference the champion's LinkedIn profile or any public information about them
- Don't use the champion angle as the sole value proposition - it's a door opener, the product value must stand on its own
